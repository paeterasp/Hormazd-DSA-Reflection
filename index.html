My Engineering & Robotics Journey

A concise, engineering-focused overview of how I developed as a robotics and STEM learner.

My interest in engineering began with a simple curiosity about how things work. This curiosity grew into a focused passion for robotics when I started programming and building systems that combined mechanical design with code.

Learning Scratch in Primary 4 gave me a strong foundation in computational thinking. Concepts like loops, conditions and variables helped me understand how logic drives real-world systems.

My first hands-on robotics experience came through EV3 robots, where I saw how sensors, motors and code interact. This sparked my commitment to pursue robotics seriously.

In Primary 5, I led my school’s National Micro:bit Hackathon team. We designed a sustainable smart home prototype and won the Best Design Award. This experience strengthened my leadership, teamwork and engineering communication.

To deepen my technical skills, I completed three levels of structured robotics training—from Beginner to Advanced—within five months. This equipped me with strong fundamentals in sensors, navigation, optimisation and iterative testing.

As a senior member of my FIRST LEGO League (UNEARTHED) team, I contributed to robot coding (Pybricks), mission strategy, research and presentation. I also built a 3D simulation to help judges visualise our innovation project.

These experiences shaped my engineering mindset: break problems into smaller parts, test systematically, improve through iteration and stay calm under pressure. I am committed to growing further in a secondary school environment that values innovation, discipline and service.
